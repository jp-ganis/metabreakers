TL;DR - Listbot estimates the points cost of a warscroll by identifying key features of that warscroll, and comparing those features to the features of other warscrolls of which the points costs are already known.

Listbot breaks a warscroll down into a long list of features, such as basic stats, number of special abilities, how many keywords, the presence of certain specific keywords like Monster or Hero., and many more.

An example is that Listbot estimates that Tzaangor Skyfires should cost 180 points (90% of their in game points cost). It is comparing Skyfires to all of the other warscrolls it has ever seen, but we can illustrate some of its thinking by looking at the scroll for Tzaangor Enlightened, which has many similar features to the Skyfires scroll. The same base stats (red), some of the same attack profiles (purple), the same amount of special rules (green), and most of the same keywords (yellow).

When evaluating Sykfires, Listbot knows that Tzaangor Enlightened cost 170 points. It then sees that Skyfires are very similar, except they also have a shooting attack*, which it believes increase their value, and bumps their cost up to the 180 points we discussed above.
*This is a guess, we can't exactly tell what Listbot is thinking for its calculations, however in this case I think it's a reasonable assumption.
